My Publications

Manuscript 1: Under Review

Journal: International Review of Economics and Finance (SSCI Q1, Impact Factor: 5.6)

Developed a hybrid forecasting framework combining Kernel Ridge Regression (KRR) and N-BEATS, enhanced with Tukey's Biweight Function for outlier resistance. The model captures both nonlinear trends and temporal patterns in cryptocurrency price data. Applied to Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Ripple (2015–2025), the framework achieved strong predictive accuracy validated through Diebold-Mariano tests and KDE analysis. SHAP values were used to interpret model predictions and support practical decision-making for investors and portfolio managers.


Manuscript 2: Under Internal Review

Extended the univariate KRR–N-BEATS hybrid framework to a multivariate setting by incorporating macroeconomic indicators including gold rate, exchange rate, USD index, and crude oil prices. Tukey's Biweight Function was retained for outlier resistance, enabling the model to capture both nonlinear trends and cross-market dependencies. Applied to Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Ripple using daily data from 2015– 2025, the multivariate model significantly outperformed all standalone and univariate baselines. Results demonstrate strong practical value for investors and analysts seeking robust, data-driven tools for portfolio management and financial risk control.


Manuscript 3: Under Internal Review

Investigated the predictive power of machine learning models combined with Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for stock market return forecasting. Key macroeconomic indicators including money supply, oil prices, exchange rates, gold prices, inflation, and the Quantum Index of Manufacturing were used as input features. PCA effectively reduced noise and multicollinearity, allowing models to capture hidden economic patterns more accurately. LightGBM-PCA achieved the best performance with the lowest RMSE and MAE, outperforming all standalone models.


Manuscript 4: In Preparation

Developed a hybrid forecasting framework combining GARCH (1,1) with Temporal Convolutional Network (TCN) and WaveNet architectures for stock market price prediction. GARCH captured linear dependence and volatility clustering, while TCN and WaveNet modeled higher-order temporal dependencies using dilated convolutions. Tukey's Biweight loss function was applied for outlier resistance in a univariate setting. Applied to Karachi Stock Exchange, Euronext 100, and NYSE using daily data from 2014–2025, the hybrid outperformed all standalone and ARIMA benchmarks.


Manuscript 5: In Preparation

Extended the univariate GARCH–TCN-WaveNet hybrid to a multivariate setting by incorporating macroeconomic indicators including oil prices, gold, USD index, and interest rates alongside technical indicators. The multivariate framework captured both volatility dynamics and cross-market dependencies, significantly improving forecasting accuracy over the univariate approach. Applied to KSE, Euronext 100, and NYSE, the model achieved substantially lower RMSE and MAE values across all exchanges. Results confirm the framework as a robust and generalized tool for real-world financial forecasting.

Thesis

XAI Enhanced Time Series and Deep Learning Hybrid Model for Cryptocurrency Price Forecasting

My Master's thesis introduced a novel hybrid forecasting framework combining Kernel Ridge Regression (KRR) and Neural Basis Expansion Analysis for Time Series (N-BEATS), with Tukey's biweight function for robustness against outliers, applied to cryptocurrency price prediction. The framework was tested on Bitcoin(BTC), Ethereum(ETH), and Ripple(XRP) using daily closing prices spanning up to a decade(2015-2025), in both univariate and multivariate settings, incorporating macroeconomic variables including oil prices, gold rates, the USD index, and interest rates. The hybrid model significantly outperformed all benchmark models including LSTM, ARIMA, and individual KRR and N-BEATS models, achieving a test RMSE of 0.002 on Bitcoin in the multivariate setting, a ten-fold improvement over the univariate baseline. Model predictions were evaluated using the Diebold-Mariano(DM) test for forecast accuracy comparison and Kernel Density Estimation(KDE) for distributional analysis, while SHAP-based explainability was applied to interpret which inputs were driving the model's predictions.
